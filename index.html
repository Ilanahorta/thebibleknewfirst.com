13 Principles that will transform your relationship with money

Each one is practical, tested, and applicable today — regardless of your faith. And all of them were in Scripture all along.

01
Spiritual Map
Your bank statement doesn't lie. It is the most honest portrait of your real values — before any change, you need to look.
Matthew 6:21
02
God's Only Rival
Money is the only rival Jesus named for God. When it becomes master, you lose peace, clarity, and what truly matters.
Matthew 6:24
03
Pay First
The oldest financial strategy in the world: set aside first — before spending. It's what survives. The Bible knew it first.
Gen 14:20 · Mal 3:10
04
The Ant
The ant has no boss or obligation — yet saves during abundance. Emergency fund and habit that becomes automatic.
Proverbs 6:6–8
05
Count the Cost Before You Build
Jesus asked: did you count the cost before starting? Planning is not distrust in God. It is wisdom.
Luke 14:28–30
06
Slave to the Lender
The rich rules over the poor, and the borrower is slave to the lender. Debt is not just financial — it is a power relationship that drains your freedom.
Proverbs 22:7
07
The Servant Punished for Hoarding
The only servant punished in the Parable of the Talents was the one who buried it. God does not reward those who hoard what they receive.
Matthew 25:14–30
08
Divide Into Seven Parts
Solomon described portfolio diversification 2,900 years before the Nobel of 1990. Don't put everything in one place.
Ecclesiastes 11:2
09
The 14-Year Plan
Joseph created the first retirement plan in history. He saved 20% for 7 years of abundance to survive 7 of scarcity. The Bible already knew.
Genesis 41
10
The Hidden Entrepreneur
The woman of Proverbs 31 buys fields, analyzes them, sells with profit, and runs a business. The Bible celebrates female entrepreneurship 3,000 years ago.
Proverbs 31
11
Whoever Sows Generously
Strategic generosity doesn't impoverish — it multiplies. Science confirmed 2,000 years after Paul wrote: whoever sows little, little reaps.
2 Corinthians 9:6
12
Inheritance and Legacy
Inheritance is money. Legacy is character. The good person leaves both — and the Bible treats this as responsibility, not optional.
Proverbs 13:22
13
Content to Be
Paul learned to be content in any situation. Contentment is not resignation — it is the psychological foundation of every healthy financial decision.
Philippians 4:11
